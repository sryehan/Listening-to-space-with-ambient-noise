Core theory — interactive

Equations rendered with MathJax. Sliders show how physical parameters change the results.

In a perfectly diffuse acoustic field, the Lobkis-Weaver relation states that ensemble-averaging the cross-correlation of noise recorded at two sensors $i$ and $j$ recovers the Green's function between them. Energy normalisation in the frequency domain flattens the source spectrum:

$$\langle C_{ij}(\tau)\rangle = \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\left\{\frac{\tilde{y}_i(\omega)\,\tilde{y}_j^*(\omega)}{\sqrt{E_i\,E_j}}\right\}$$ $$\frac{d}{d\tau}\langle C_{ij}(\tau)\rangle \;\approx\; -G(\mathbf{r}_i,\mathbf{r}_j,\tau)\;+\;G(\mathbf{r}_j,\mathbf{r}_i,-\tau)$$
Time derivative of the cross-correlation recovers the causal + anti-causal Green's function. SNR improves as $\sqrt{N_r}$ with the number of realisations $N_r$.
Realisations $N_r$50
Sensor distance $r$ (m)0.52 m
—

For a uniform linear array of $N$ sensors with spacing $d$, the Bartlett beamformer output power at steering angle $\theta$ is the squared magnitude of the coherent sum of phase-shifted sensor signals. In the near field ($R \ll R_F = 2L^2/\lambda$) the plane-wave model fails; a spherical steering vector must be used instead:

$$P_{\text{BF}}(\theta) = \left|\sum_{i=0}^{N-1} e^{-jkd\,i\sin\theta}\right|^2, \qquad k = \frac{2\pi f}{c}$$ $$w_i^{\text{PS}}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}_i|}\,e^{-jk|\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}_i|}$$
Bartlett (far-field) vs. point-source (near-field) steering vectors. $-3\,\text{dB}$ beamwidth: $\Delta\theta \approx 2\arcsin(0.886\,\lambda/L)$.
Elements $N$14
Frequency $f$ (kHz)5.0 kHz
Pitch $d$ (cm)5.0 cm

Full waveform inversion recovers the squared slowness $m(\mathbf{x}) = 1/c(\mathbf{x})^2$ by minimising the $\ell^2$ data residual. The gradient is computed cheaply by correlating the forward wavefield $u$ (from source) with the adjoint wavefield $q$ (backpropagated residual):

$$J(m) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{s}\bigl\|d_s^{\text{obs}} - d_s^{\text{calc}}(m)\bigr\|^2$$ $$\frac{\partial J}{\partial m}\bigg|_{\mathbf{x}} = \sum_{\omega,s}\omega^2\,\operatorname{Re}\!\bigl[u_s(\mathbf{x},\omega)\cdot q_s^*(\mathbf{x},\omega)\bigr]$$
Adjoint-state method — gradient at cost of 2 forward solves per source. The kernel has the characteristic banana-doughnut shape of the Fresnel zone.
Velocity perturbation $\Delta c/c$+10 %
Noise level $\sigma$0.05

Lab I · 16 December 2025 · Passive Imaging

Passive Imaging of a Scattering Cylinder using Ambient Noise

Green's function retrieval from diffuse noise via cross-correlation. Delay-and-Sum beamforming with differential imaging to locate a hidden aluminium cylinder inside a 32-microphone circular array.

Full report (PDF)
↗ Open PDF in new tab

Lobkis-Weaver cross-correlation. Energy-normalised cross-spectrum between sensors $i$ and $j$:

$$\langle C_{ij}(\tau)\rangle = \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\left\{\frac{\tilde{y}_i(\omega)\,\tilde{y}_j^*(\omega)}{\sqrt{E_i E_j}}\right\} \;\longrightarrow\; \partial_\tau\langle C_{ij}\rangle \approx -G(\mathbf{r}_i,\mathbf{r}_j,\tau)+G(\mathbf{r}_j,\mathbf{r}_i,-\tau)$$
Direct travel-time peak confirmed at $\tau_0 \approx 1.3\,\text{ms}$ (Mic 1 – Mic 9, diameter $\approx 0.52\,\text{m}$, $c=343\,\text{m/s}$).

Delay-and-Sum image. TFM sum over all 32×32 sensor pairs:

$$I(x,y) = \sum_{i=1}^{M}\sum_{j=1}^{M}\left|G\!\left(\mathbf{r}_i,\mathbf{r}_j,\,\frac{d_i+d_j}{c}\right)\right|, \qquad I_{\text{diff}} = \bigl|I_{\text{cyl}} - I_{\text{empty}}\bigr|$$
Differential imaging suppresses direct-path inter-sensor contributions, leaving only the cylinder's scattering contribution. Detected position: $X = -0.03\,\text{m}$, $Y = 0.00\,\text{m}$.

Lab II · 30 December 2025 · Source Localisation

Acoustic Source Localisation using a Linear Array

14-element MEMS linear array. Near-field spherical-wave beamforming, time-gating against reflections, and Eigenvector noise-subspace filtering in a reverberant room.

Full report (PDF)
↗ Open PDF in new tab

Near-field criterion. With aperture $L = (N-1)d = 0.65\,\text{m}$ and $f_c = 5\,\text{kHz}$:

$$R_F = \frac{2L^2}{\lambda} = \frac{2\times(0.65)^2}{343/5000} \approx 12.3\,\text{m} \;\gg\; R_{\text{source}} \approx 1\,\text{m}$$
Source is deeply in the near field — plane-wave model (Bartlett) estimates direction only; point-source model recovers both range and direction.

Steering vectors. Far-field (plane wave) vs. near-field (spherical):

$$w_i^{\text{PW}}(\theta) = e^{-jkd\,i\sin\theta}, \qquad w_i^{\text{PS}}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{1}{|\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}_i|}\,e^{-jk|\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}_i|}$$
Point-source model recovered source at $(X,Y) \approx (-0.08, 0.98)\,\text{m}$. Time-gating suppressed ±60° reflection sidelobes.

Lab III · 2025 · Full Waveform Inversion

Acoustic FWI — Adjoint-State Method

Iterative 2D velocity model recovery by minimising an $\ell^2$ waveform misfit. Gradient via adjoint-state equations, L-BFGS optimiser, multi-scale frequency strategy.

Full report (PDF)
↗ Open PDF in new tab

Misfit and adjoint-state gradient. FWI solves the Helmholtz equation $(\nabla^2 + \omega^2 m)\,u = b$ and minimises:

$$J(m) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{s}\bigl\|d_s^{\text{obs}} - \mathbf{E}_{\text{rec}}\,u_s(m)\bigr\|^2$$ $$\frac{\partial J}{\partial m}\bigg|_{\mathbf{x}} = \sum_{\omega,s}\omega^2\,\operatorname{Re}\!\bigl[u_s(\mathbf{x},\omega)\cdot q_s^*(\mathbf{x},\omega)\bigr]$$
Adjoint source: $b_q = \mathbf{E}_{\text{rec}}(u_s\big|_{\text{rec}} - d_s^{\text{obs}})$; adjoint wavefield $q_s$ solved via the transposed system $\mathbf{A}^T q_s = b_q$. Cost: 2 forward solves per source — no Jacobian needed.
